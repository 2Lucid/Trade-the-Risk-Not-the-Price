Trade the Risk,
Not the Price.

A research project by Clément Bellet-Odent

You can't predict whether the market rises or falls tomorrow. But you can predict how turbulent it will be — and that is enough to earn a better risk-adjusted return. Move the sliders below and re-run a real buy-side strategy, live, on 26 years of global data.

The starting point

The price is noise. The risk is not.

Out-of-sample, no model beats a simple historical average at forecasting next month's return (R² is negative). The same exercise on next month's volatility works strikingly well. That single asymmetry is the entire strategy.

Does it travel?

Across the world, and across asset classes.

Volatility timing alone is mostly an equity phenomenon — honest, and exactly what the academic literature finds. But adding a trend overlay and diversifying across markets is where the real, durable edge lives.

Diversified portfolio, vol-matched

Why it works

An algorithm rediscovered the crises — with no dates.

A Hidden Markov Model, fed only returns, splits history into calm and turbulent regimes on its own. It shades the dot-com bust, 2008, COVID and 2022 in red — the very moments the strategy cuts risk.

Under the hood

How the Lab computes.

1

Forecast the risk

Estimate next month's variance from the past (last month's realized variance, or a GARCH model). Never using future data.

2

Size the position

Invest inversely to forecast variance: more when calm, less when turbulent. An optional trend signal sets the direction.

3

Match the risk

Scale the whole thing to the same long-run volatility as buy-and-hold, so the Sharpe comparison is fair.

4

Charge reality

Cap the leverage and subtract a trading cost on every change in position. Then measure Sharpe, return and drawdown.

⚠️ This is a research and educational tool, not investment advice. A backtest is not live trading; results depend on the sample, assume frictionless leverage up to the cap, and past performance does not predict future results.
